Feature Explanations

Understanding these features is crucial for interpreting the model's predictions:

cp (Chest Pain Type)

The type of chest pain experienced by the patient. This is often one of the most important indicators for heart disease.

  • Value 0: Typical angina
  • Value 1: Atypical angina
  • Value 2: Non-anginal pain
  • Value 3: Asymptomatic
thalach (Maximum Heart Rate)

The maximum heart rate achieved during exercise. Lower maximum heart rates may indicate cardiovascular issues.

ca (Number of Major Vessels)

The number of major vessels (0-3) colored by fluoroscopy. More colored vessels generally indicate more severe heart disease.

thal (Thalassemia)

A blood disorder called thalassemia that can affect heart health:

  • Value 0: Normal
  • Value 1: Fixed defect
  • Value 2: Reversible defect
oldpeak (ST Depression)

ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest. Higher values can indicate heart issues.
